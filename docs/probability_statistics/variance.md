## Variance

Variance measures how spread out values are around the mean.

It is the mathematical version of spread.

Because variance uses squared distances, it is very useful mathematically, but not always easy to read directly.

## Standard deviation

Standard deviation is the square root of variance.

It describes spread in the original unit of the data, so it is more human-readable.

## Population variance

Population variance is used when we have the full group or full world we care about.

It measures how much the complete population varies.

## Sample variance

Sample variance is used when we only have part of the world.

It estimates how much the full population varies based on a smaller sample.

Sample variance means:

I only saw part of the world, but I want to estimate how much the whole world varies.


*** Population variance = I have the full world I care about.
* Sample variance = I have a small window, and I use it to estimate the full world.**

## Why sample variance matters

Very often the whole reality is unavailable, too large, too expensive, not finished yet, or not even fully defined.

In machine learning, even when we have a dataset, we often treat it as only a sample of future reality.

## Important distinction

“I already have the world, and I want to study how random groups behave inside it.”

That is a valid question, but it is different from sample variance.

Sample variance is not mainly about studying random groups inside a known world.

It is about using a smaller observed part to estimate the larger world we cannot fully see.


**Variance is the mathematical version of spread; standard deviation is the human-readable version of the same spread.

Population variance studies the full world you have; sample variance estimates the full world from the small window you saw.**
